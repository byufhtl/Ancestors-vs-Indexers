What is this?

Ancestors vs Indexers is a free family history game developed by the Brigham Young University Family History Technology Lab. The game is designed to provide a fun incentive for people of all ages to get involved in their family histories.

Ancestors vs Indexers provides a way for users to turn their family history work into a game. It uses data obtained from FamilySearch (familysearch.org) to generate a series of secenarios in which a player must use various kinds of tools and resources to stop their lost ancestors from progressing from one end of the playing field to the other. Data is obtained from FamilySearch according to our Privacy Statement.

The game simulates the time which we have every day to perform family history work and record our family history before it grows to late. This will require the efforts of many people from many nations, cultures, and ethnic backgrounds.

Who are you guys?

The BYU Family History Technology Lab is a research lab sponsored by Brigham Young University. The lab's purpose is to develop technologies that advance family history by providing new tools to enable, encourage, and excite people about their genealogies. Learn more at fhtl.byu.edu
